--- title: "Methodology" layout: "page" draft: false toc: true hideMeta: true --- --- ## Purpose Dead Air Project follows a **documentation-first** approach. The goal is not to validate beliefs or disprove skepticism, but to create a transparent, repeatable framework for recording, reviewing, and archiving anomalous audio. No assumptions are made about the origin of recorded sounds. --- ## Session structure Each session follows a loosely standardized structure to ensure consistency: 1. **Environment** - Location is noted - Time of day is recorded - Ambient conditions are observed (silence, background noise, electronics) 2. **Recording** - Raw audio is captured using one or more devices - No live filtering or processing during recording - Sessions may include controlled silence periods 3. **Ghostbox / ITC (when used)** - Radio sweep rate and method are not modified mid-session - No real-time interpretation is spoken during recording - Questions, if asked, are neutral and non-leading --- ## Audio handling ### Raw recordings Raw audio is preserved in its original form and always presented first. Raw recordings represent the primary source material. ### Enhanced reference audio Enhanced versions may be created using: - gain adjustment - equalization - noise reduction No content is added or synthesized. Enhanced audio exists solely as a **listening aid** and may introduce artefacts. For this reason, raw audio should always be considered the authoritative source. --- ## EVP markers & interpretation Markers are placed at timestamps where the listener perceives: - voice-like elements - anomalous sounds - responses following prompts Markers include: - **Type** (whisper / response / noise) - **Confidence level** (low / medium / high) These labels reflect **subjective perception**, not conclusions. Different listeners may hear different things — or nothing at all. --- ## Transparency - No sessions are edited to support a narrative - No markers are added retroactively without disclosure - Equipment and settings are documented Data is presented so that **independent interpretation is possible**. --- ## Limitations Dead Air Project acknowledges several inherent limitations: - human pattern recognition bias - audio artefacts caused by processing - environmental interference - radio frequency bleed-through These limitations are part of the documentation, not something to be hidden. Note: Audio files are hosted on a separate media domain. Some corporate or restricted networks may block direct media access. --- > *Methodology does not reveal truth — > it only defines how observations are made.*
